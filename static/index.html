About Us

We are Auburn University’s first-ever programming event organized by students, for students. With this hackathon, Auburn University's Computer Science & Software Engineering strives to promote technical innovation and highlight students’ skills and abilities. Partnering with Major League Hacking (MLH) for the first time, we aim to bring out the best and brightest students, not just from Auburn University but from other universities all over the United States.

On February 2, 2019, this 24-hour, full-steam-ahead event will challenge participants to learn, think outside the box, and push the limits on what they can build using technology.

Information

What is a hackathon? Are we hacking anything?

We are not "hackers" as you would normally think of them. Hacking, in this context, involves working on a team (or alone, if you wish!) to put together a website, application, etc. This will be a 24-hour event that will encourage you to innovate and build something cool, or just learn something new, in the span of a weekend.

When is AuburnHacks?

The event will take place on the weekend of February 2 (Saturday), 2019 and conclude the following Sunday afternoon.

Where is AuburnHacks?

It will be in the Auburn University Student Center Ballroom, located at 255 Heisman Dr, Auburn, AL 36849. Parking will be available in the parking deck next to the building (see below).

What do I need to bring?

Bring your computer and whatever else you will need to code! Also, since it is an overnight event, bring a toothbrush as well as a sleeping bag! We will provide food, drinks, and caffeine, so you can focus on programming!

What if I don't know how to code?

No worries at all! We welcome participants of all majors and grade levels. Everyone can bring something to the table, and if you just want to hang out and learn from our mentors, you may do so as well! Hackathons are a learning experience for everyone, so AuburnHacks will encourage that type of environment.
