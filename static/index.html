About Us

We are Auburn University’s first-ever programming event organized by students, for students. With this hackathon, Auburn University's Computer Science & Software Engineering strives to promote technical innovation and highlight students’ skills and abilities. Partnering with Major League Hacking (MLH) for the first time, we aim to bring out the best and brightest students, not just from Auburn University but from other universities all over the United States.

On February 2, 2019, this 24-hour, full-steam-ahead event will challenge participants to learn, think outside the box, and push the limits on what they can build using technology.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

We are not "hackers" as you would normally think of them. Hacking, in this context, involves working on a team (or alone, if you wish!) to put together a website, application, etc. This will be a 24-hour event that will encourage you to innovate and build something cool, or just learn something new.

When is AuburnHacks?

The event will commence on Saturday, February 2, 2019 and conclude the following Sunday afternoon. Specific details will be announced later.

Where is AuburnHacks?

It will be in the Auburn University Student Center Ballroom, located at 255 Heisman Dr, Auburn, AL 36849. Parking will be available in the parking deck next to the building (see map below).

What do I need to bring?

Bring your computer and whatever else you will need to code! Also, since it is an overnight event, bring a toothbrush as well as a sleeping bag! We will provide food, drinks, and caffeine, so you can focus on programming!

How much does it cost?

It's completely free to attend and participate! Just like any other Major League Hacking Event!

What if I don't know how to code?

No worries at all! We welcome participants of all majors and grade levels. Even if you just want to hang out and learn from our mentors, you may do so! Hackathons are a learning experience for everyone, so AuburnHacks will encourage that type of environment.

What if I don't have a team?

You don't need a team to participate, although we do encourage it! If you don't have a team coming into the event, we will provide avenues for you to form a team onsite. Details will be provided on the day of the event.

What will the prompt be?

We'll announce the hacking challenges when the event kicks off. Rest assured, we will have many different challenges to tackle, so if one doesn't capture your interest or skill level, you can try your hand at something else.

Anything else I should know?

Major League Hacking has a code of conduct! We want everyone to be safe and comfortable at AuburnHacks. Before attending our event, please review the document here.
